1. The Starting Lineup (The 7 Hires)
Selecting for speed, sensory power, and physical versatility.
•	Bat (Aerial Recon): Navigates pitch-black, narrow voids using echolocation to find air pockets where survivors might be trapped.
•	Cockroach (Deep Rubble Scout): Its flattened body allows it to enter collapsed slabs that no other team member can reach. High resistance to toxins/dust.
•	Elephant (Heavy Extraction & Triage): The "muscle." Capable of lifting concrete beams. Its high empathy is used for "Psychological Triage" to calm survivors during extraction.
•	Dolphin (Aquatic Lead): Essential for flooded basements or subway tunnels. Uses sonar to "see" through murky water.
•	Starfish (Multi-Directional Medic): Can climb vertical or inverted rubble with ease. If a limb is trapped and lost, it regenerates, ensuring the medic stays in the field.
•	Wolf (Tactical Coordinator): The pack leader. Manages the "supply chain" of information and ensures the team covers the grid efficiently without overlapping.
•	Beaver (Structural Engineer): While others find survivors, the Beaver builds immediate wooden or debris-based shoring to prevent the "pancake effect" of further collapse.
2. The Workspace & Interactive Design
How the building supports the biology.
•	The Echo-Chamber (Ceiling): A textured, sound-dampening ceiling with hanging "roosting bars" for the Bat to rest without noise interference.
•	The Glass Conduit System: Transparent micro-tunnels running through the walls, allowing the Cockroach to travel between rooms instantly.
•	The Triage Bay: A 10-meter deep filtered water tank connected to the building's structural frame, allowing the Dolphin to communicate with the team via vibrations.
•	Reinforced Concrete Slabs: Floors designed to withstand $10,000$ kg of pressure for the Elephant, featuring "Rumble Mats" for low-frequency communication.
4. HR Remedies & Accommodations
•	Fixing biological weaknesses with technology.
Animal	Biological Weakness	Technical/Policy Remedy
Bat	Fragile Membranes: Wings are easily torn by jagged rebar or broken glass in rubble.	Kevlar Wing-Guards: Ultra-light, flexible ballistic mesh fitted over the wings to prevent puncture wounds during flight.
Cockroach	Pest Perception: Risk of being accidentally crushed by larger teammates or survivors.	LED Beacon Shells: A microscopic, adhesive LED tracker attached to their back so the Wolf can track their position on a digital map.
Elephant	Massive Caloric Drain: Requires hundreds of pounds of food daily to maintain strength for debris clearing.	On-Site Hydroponic Silo: A 24/7 vertical farm within the HQ to provide high-protein vegetation "fuel" without logistical delays.
Dolphin	Aquatic Limitation: Cannot navigate dry land or move between disconnected flooded pockets.	"Hydra-Suit" Exoskeleton: A moisture-locking suit that keeps skin/gills hydrated, fitted with robotic "flippers" for short-term land mobility.
Starfish	Slow Locomotion: While they can climb anything, they are slow over long distances.	Rapid Deployment Drones: The Bat or Owl carries the Starfish to the specific "triage point" to save time.
Wolf	Sensory Overload: High-frequency noise from sirens and machinery can cause panic or hearing loss.	Tactical Ear-Pro: Noise-canceling headsets that filter out mechanical "clatter" while amplifying the heartbeat of a survivor.
Beaver	Dental Wear: Constant gnawing and shoring of heavy timber/debris can lead to tooth structural failure.	Titanium Dental Caps: Reinforcements for the incisors to ensure they can cut through heavy structural materials without injury.
5. Future Expansion
Securing the next hire.
•	Next Hire: The Owl.
•	Why? While the Bat is good in tight spaces, the Owl provides silent long-range night vision. With more funding, we need an "Eye in the Sky" that can spot heat signatures from 500 feet up without the noise of a helicopter.

6. The "Do Not Hire" List
The fatal flaw.
•	Animal: The Sloth.
•	Why? Disaster response is a race against time. The Sloth’s top speed of 0.27 km/h would cause mission failure. They move too slowly to provide "immediate triage" and would physically block the paths of the faster responders like the Wolf or Elephant.
